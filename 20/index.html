Continue frying the chicken in batches

Warning: this homemade pineapple-teriyaki glaze is addicting. Serve any extra on the side for dipping.
Total Time: 1:00
Level: Moderate
Serves: 4
Ingredients

    ? c. butter
    4 clove garlic
    1 tbsp. fresh ginger
    ? c. soy sauce
    ? c. sake
    ? c. pineapple juice
    ? c. rice vinegar
    2 tbsp. brown sugar
    2 tbsp. water
    1 tbsp. cornstarch
    2 lb. chicken wings
    kosher or sea salt
    Fresh cracked black pepper
    1 c. all-purpose flour
    Vegetable oil (or other high flashpoint oil)
    green onions

Directions

    Heat a saucepan over medium heat. Melt the butter and stir in the garlic and ginger. Cook for 1 to 2 minutes, or until the garlic is lightly browned nu skin.
    Add the soy sauce, sake, pineapple juice, rice vinegar, and brown sugar. Bring up to a gentle boil and simmer for 3 minutes.
    Whisk the water and the cornstarch together and then whisk mixture into the teriyaki sauce and simmer until thickened, 8 to 10 minutes. Set aside nuskin.
    Rinse the chicken wings and pat them dry. Generously season the chicken with salt and black pepper. Dredge the wings in flour, shaking off any excess flour, and set aside.
    In a large saucepan or deep fryer, heat the oil to about 375 degrees F. Fry the chicken wings in small batches until golden brown, 8 to 10 minutes. Shake off any excess oil and place on paper towels to drain.  until all of the wings are cooked.
    Toss the wings in the teriyaki sauce and sprinkle with green onions. Serve warm.
